ABOUT
"A Girl Who Can Do It All"
Taylor Swift was born in West Reading, Pennsylvania. When she was 13, her parents sold their family farm in Pennsylvania and moved to Hendersonville, Tennessee, so she could pursue a career in country music in nearby Nashville.
In 2004, at age 14, Taylor Swift signed a music publishing deal with Sony/ATV, thereby becoming the youngest signing in the company's history. In 2006 Swift signed with Big Machine Records and scored her first Top 40 hit with “Tim McGraw.” She then released four more singles and a self-titled album.
16 long years of her career were more like 16 years of encountering challenges and struggles but then overcoming them. The 32 year old is more than just a musician at this point. She is a businesswoman, a director, an actress and pretty much a household name. Inspiring many others, she has become an icon to look up to.

MILESTONES
Won 11 grammys in multiple categories and bagged a total of 42 nominations. She was once the youngest artist ever to win a grammy for album of the year and is now the first and only woman solo artist to win a grammy for album of the year thrice for her solo recordings.
Recieved an Honorary Doctorate of Fine Arts by New York University for her outstanding achievements in the music industry. NYU's Clive Davis Institute of Recorded Music even has a course dediacted to Taylor's music, business and writing.
She selflessly fought for the rights of artists. She even pulled her entire music catalog off Spotify in protest in 2014. Her whole catalog was sold to somebody else without her knowledge and she not only earned them all back by re-recording all her sold music but also continued to stand up for other artists facing the same issues.
Swift has made over 50 world records in her career to date including most AMA wins, most singles to debut in top 10 of the hot 100, fastest selling albums and much more.
